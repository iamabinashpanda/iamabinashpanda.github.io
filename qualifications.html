Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions people ask me — answered for your convenience.

1. What are your short-term and long-term goals?

In the short term, I aim to deepen my expertise in DevOps and cloud-native architectures. In the long term, I aspire to take on technical leadership roles where I can design scalable solutions and mentor junior developers.

2. What were you doing during the gap after your graduation?

After completing my B.Tech in 2019, the COVID-19 pandemic hit, which delayed opportunities for many fresh graduates. During that time, I focused on upskilling myself by completing a Web Development course in PHP, Laravel, and MySQLi, where I built a small eCommerce proof-of-concept project. This phase helped me strengthen my technical foundation and prepare for my professional journey.

3. What caused the gap after your last professional experience?

I faced severe health challenges that required dedicated recovery time and medical care. I had facial paralysis, followed by a pilonidal surgery, which made mobility and day-to-day work extremely difficult for nearly a year.During this period, I tried to stay connected with learning—enrolling in an online course (completed around 20%)—but had to pause it temporarily due to health constraints.Now that I’m fully recovered and have made key lifestyle adjustments, I’m eager and fit to rejoin the workforce with renewed focus and energy.

4. How did you stay connected with your field during recovery?

Even though I couldn’t work full-time, I continued exploring DevOps and cloud technologies through online resources, documentation, and community discussions. This helped me stay familiar with emerging tools and practices, so I could smoothly transition back into professional work once my health stabilized.

5. How do you view these career gaps now?

Those periods were both challenging and transformative. They taught me resilience, patience, and self-management. I’ve emerged with a stronger mindset, improved discipline, and deeper appreciation for both personal health and professional growth.

6. Are you ready to resume full-time professional work now?

Yes, I’m fully recovered and ready to take on new challenges. I’m currently focusing on remote opportunities, which allow me to maintain consistency and deliver my best performance.I completely understand the value of both time and compensation, and I’m not rigid about negotiations — my priority is to contribute meaningfully and grow with the organization. I’m fully committed to delivering 100% productivity and accountability, ensuring quality outcomes, and maintaining a healthy work–life balance that supports sustained performance and professional excellence.
